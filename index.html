SDG 15

LIFE ON LAND

SDG 15 focuses on terrestrial ecosystems to prevent harmful practices and ensure the availability of essential resources like food, water, and shelter, vital for human survival.

LEARN MORE

OVERVIEW

SDG 15 aims to maintain, restore, and foster the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, manage forests sustainably, prevent and rectify land degradation, combat desertification, and stop biodiversity loss. To preserve various forms of life on land, specific efforts must be made to maintain, restore, and promote terrestrial and other ecosystem conservation and sustainable usage. Goal 15 focuses on sustainable forest management, reducing and stopping land and natural habitat deterioration, successfully battling desertification, and preventing biodiversity loss. The goal of all of these initiatives is to guarantee that future generations will be able to enjoy the many benefits of land-based ecosystems, including sustainable livelihoods.

PROBLEM

Deforestation, driven by illegal logging, urbanization, and agricultural expansion, poses a significant challenge to achieving SDG 15. Illegal logging depletes forests and biodiversity, while rapid urbanization clears large areas of natural habitats for development. Additionally, agriculture—particularly large-scale farming and livestock grazing—leads to significant forest loss as land is cleared for crops and pasture. These activities destroy ecosystems and accelerate climate change and biodiversity loss, making sustainable land management critical to addressing these issues.

SDG 15 STATISTICS

Annual Land Degradation

Between 2015 and 2019, at least 100 million hectares of productive land were degraded each year globally.

Philippine Land Degradation in 2019

In 2019, 14.26 million hectares of land, or 49.04% of the Philippines' total land area, were degraded.

Impact on Filipino Population

Land degradation affects 42.92 million people in the Philippines, or 41.54% of the population, leading to adverse effects.

Soil Erosion in Agriculture

Soil erosion affects 75% of the Philippines' agricultural cropland, with 457 million tons of soil lost annually.

Forests' Global Livelihood Support

Forests provide livelihoods for over 1.6 billion people worldwide.

Biodiversity in Forests

Over 80% of all terrestrial animal, plant, and insect species inhabit forests.

Land Degradation and Poverty

Globally, 74% of the underprivileged population is directly impacted by land degradation.

Animal Breeds at Risk

Of the 8,300 recognized animal breeds, 8% are extinct, and 22% are at risk of extinction.

Accelerated Land and Agricultural Loss

Land degradation and agricultural loss are occurring 30 to 35 times faster than historical rates, affecting 12 million hectares annually.

Rising Desertification

Desertification, coupled with drought, is increasingly leading to the annual loss of 12 million hectares of land globally, disproportionately affecting disadvantaged communities.

GOALS

Promote Ecosystem Conservation and Restoration

Promote the conservation, restoration, and sustainable use of terrestrial and inland freshwater ecosystems and their services, with a focus on forests, wetlands, mountains, and drylands, in line with obligations under international agreements.

Protect Mountain Ecosystems

Preserve mountain ecosystems and their biodiversity to enhance their ability to deliver benefits for sustainable development.

Halt Biodiversity Loss

Take urgent and significant action to reduce the degradation of natural habitats, halt the loss of biodiversity.

Combat Poaching and Trafficking

Enhance global support to combat poaching and trafficking of protected species including by increasing the capacity of local communities to pursue sustainable livelihood opportunities.

World Wildlife Fund

In order to maintain the richness of nature, improve our climate, and maintain human well-being, we must preserve the world's forests by 2030. WWF is working to solve these threats to forests. The majority of WWF's work takes place in tropical rainforests, which are the most biologically diverse and intricate ecosystems on the planet—forests in the Amazon, Congo Basin, Greater Mekong, and other equatorial regions. However, it is also taking place in temperate locations like the Russian Far East and the United States.

The International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN)

The IUCN advocates for strategies that link sustainable land, water, and biodiversity management to human health and well-being. IUCN provides resources such as the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species, the IUCN Red List of Ecosystems, and the Integrated Biodiversity Assessment Tool to help conservation programs, businesses, and national and international policymakers. IUCN’s goal is to protect and restore species and ecosystems, as well as secure the ecosystem services that civilization relies on.

Conservation International (CI)

The protection of the Earth's climate, ecosystems, and biodiversity is critical to the well-being of all people. Conservation International works worldwide to enhance people's lives by safeguarding seas, forests, and other natural habitats.

Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS)

The World Conservation Society's objective is to protect the world's greatest wild places in 14 priority regions, which provide habitat for almost half of the world's biodiversity and a wide range of distinctive large mammals.

Plant-for-the-Planet

Plant-for-the-Planet is a global initiative that aims to restore forest ecosystems in order to combat climate change and biodiversity loss .Plant-for-the-Planet’s empower youth, restore ecosystems, perform restoration research, and provide free software tools and restoration guidance to institutions all over the world.

Nature-based Solutions (NbS)

Reforestation and Afforestation

Planting trees to repair degraded areas and forests is a key trend in reforestation and afforestation. By 2030, 350 million hectares of damaged land are expected to be restored thanks to initiatives like the Bonn Challenge.

Agroforestry

Including trees in agricultural systems helps to preserve biodiversity, improve soil health, and increase carbon sequestration. It's starting to gain popularity as a sustainable land management method.

Wetland Restoration

Rehabilitating wetlands to maintain water quality, provide habitats for species, and act as natural buffers against floods.

Technology Innovation Trends

Drones

Drones are being utilized for monitoring wildlife and their habitats as well as targeted reforestation, especially in remote locations.

Sustainable Land Management

Regenerative Agriculture

Farming practices that restore soil health, increase biodiversity, and improve water cycles are gaining popularity. This includes no-till farming, crop rotation, and cover cropping.

Education Programs

Environmental Education Programs

Programs to teach the value of biodiversity, conservation, and sustainable land use are becoming more and more popular in schools and communities.

Private Sectors

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) Initiatives

Companies are aligning their CSR efforts with SDG 15, encouraging sustainable practices and sponsoring conservation projects.

Diverse Perspectives: Shaping the Future of Sustainable Cities

Climate Change

Climate change poses the most significant threat to sustainable cities. Rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and sea-level rise threaten urban infrastructure, public health, and the livelihoods of city dwellers.

Resource Depletion

Cities consume a lot of water, energy, and land, which puts pressure on these resources. Sustainable cities require smart solutions to manage their use and reduce waste.

Social Inequality

Rapid urban growth often results in social inequality, with poorer communities experiencing higher levels of poverty, limited access to services, and environmental risks. Sustainable cities should prioritize fairness and ensure that everyone has access to opportunities and a healthy environment.

Urban Development

Uncontrolled urban sprawl uses up valuable land, increases transportation costs, and damages the environment. Sustainable cities need thoughtful planning to manage growth and preserve natural areas.

Satelligence helps businesses address deforestation and fulfill sustainability commitments by utilizing satellite geodata analytics. In order to track deforestation and environmental risks in real-time, patterns and trends in agriculture and forests are assessed by combining radar and optical satellite images.

Drylet

Houston, USA

Drylet offers and supports bioremediation systems for the energy, swine, and municipal and agricultural wastewater treatment sectors. Their biocatalyst technology, which increases aerobic digestion process efficiency and enhances project economics and operations, is used to achieve this.

ReNature

Amsterdam, Netherlands

ReNature seeks to alter the production process in order to establish sustainable agroforestry systems. The company helps local government agencies, cooperatives, and farmers create sustainable agriculture through agroforestry by designing and implementing model farms and building model schools.

Virotec

Arundel, Australia

Virotec is an expert in waste management and environmental remediation. It offers technology and solutions to tackle difficult issues, like hazardous waste, and transform them into valuable and usable resources.

NanoFex

New Orleans, USA

NanoFex focuses on developing nanomaterials for the environmental remediation sector. The business creates and manufactures cellulose nanospheres using a specially designed spray dryer process to create a powdered nanoparticle that can efficiently and affordably remove chlorinated solvents from surfaces.

Nexom

Winnipeg, Canada

Nexom is a wastewater treatment company that develops and sells its own proprietary technologies for cleaner water. These biological or filtration-based technologies enable new and existing wastewater treatment plants to meet nutrient reduction targets while also meeting extremely stringent discharge standards.

FlowWorks

Seattle, USA

FlowWorks' web-based tools enable environmental monitoring, as well as real-time reporting and analytics. They collect, manage, and analyze environmental data, converting it into useful information.

Satelytics

Toledo, The United States

Satelytics is a cloud-based geospatial analytics software suite that enables businesses to track their assets on the ground and receive notifications on crucial situations. The company detects hydrocarbon leaks, water quality, vegetation health, and other issues utilizing data from sources such as satellites, drones, or planes, as well as its analytical software.

Livestock Water Recycling

Calgary, Canada

Livestock Water Recycling is a global manufacturer of manure treatment systems for hog, dairy, and anaerobic digester applications. Using its unique manure treatment method, the company recycles clean water and fertilizer nutrients from livestock waste for use on the farm.

Porifera

San Leandro, The United States

Porifera creates innovative membranes and systems for water purification and product concentration. This allows them to concentrate juices, wines, coffees, and other liquids and aqueous solutions by removing water and preserving only the product's most valuable components.

Land Life Company

Amsterdam, The Netherlands

Land Life Company is a technology-driven reforestation company that provides enterprises and organizations with a sustainable way to address climate change and offset carbon emissions by planting trees on a large scale. This is accomplished by incorporating data, drones, artificial intelligence, and monitoring applications throughout the planting process.

Collective Crunch

Espoo, Finland

Collective Crunch analyzes climate, geospatial, and process data in their SaaS platform to provide deeper insights and forest inventory projections, assisting businesses with forest sales, valuation, and management.

Komaza

Kilifi, Kenya

Komaza is a distributed forestry company that delivers technology, services, and training to smallholder farms in Africa, resulting in new sustainable wood supply. By utilizing farmer land and labor, Komaza can effectively access endless acreage and plant trees for much less, while also providing training, planting input, maintenance assistance, and a guaranteed market for wood processing and sales activities.

Dendra

Oxford, The United Kingdom

Dendra Systems employs data science, artificial intelligence, and automated drones with a bespoke seed-spitter to rehabilitate land and reestablish biodiverse ecosystems on a large scale. This is accomplished by recognizing difficult places and land changes that may jeopardize restoration efforts and applying specific seed mixes via aerial seeding.

Tesselo

Alcabideche, Portugal

Tesselo improves land resource management by combining satellite imagery, artificial intelligence, and sectoral expertise. This yields real-time, country-wide mapping solutions applicable to industries such as forestry, agriculture, infrastructure, and cities.

Vestaron

Kalamazoo, The United States

Vestaron provides growers with innovative, peptide-based chemicals that address existing resistance challenges. They manufacture pesticides for agriculture, animal health, non-crop, and commercial pest management applications using their patented peptide optimisation platform and fermentation-based peptide synthesis process.

CoverCress

St. Louis, The United States

CoverCress is an oilseed crop produced in the winter between full-season corn and soybeans. The pennycress-based crop has low amounts of erucic, fiber, and sinigrin, despite the limits of native pennycress. As a result, it generates oil and high protein feed while also serving as a cover crop, improving soil and producing grain without displacing other crops.

Plenty

San Francisco, The United States

Plenty's small field-scale indoor vertical farms aim to offer fresh, local produce to communities around the country in a more environmentally friendly manner. They provide an ideal environment for plants while mitigating the unpredictability of climate change. As a result, buyers receive pesticide-free, sustainable produce.
